BAMBI!

✿ Common name: Ginseng Ficus (Bonsai)

✿ Botanical name: Ficus retusa

✿ Plant type: Tree, evergreen

✿ Sun exposure required: Full

✿ Water care: Water when soil gets dry, mist plant daily

✿ Native area: Asia

09.03.2023

On September 3rd, Bambi was picked up from IKEA! My first plant adoption while living here in New York.


It was love at first sight… After seeing her lovely fluid roots I knew I had to adopt her and bring her home. The name Bambi came almost immediately, the way her roots raise her slightly above the ground makes her look like a deer creature with her leaves as her antlers. The space below her creates this hidden nook underneath, perfect for a little creature to rest in.


Bambi’s species, the Ginseng Ficus, is native to Southeast Asia just like me! She thrives in sunlight and fortunately is very easy for a newbie plant owner to take care of. She also loves to stay moist and doesn’t like to be under-watered.


Adopting Bambi is a symbolic start of my new era here in New York City, we are both in it for the long ride and to adjust to this new yet exciting environment together!

09.17.2023

Unfortunately she has been losing quite a few leaves now and then ;-; Maybe it’s the new environment? I’ll need to keep an eye out for that.


I think something I definitely need to do next is to repot Bambi, this pot is too small for her roots to grow further.

10.28.2023

Lol very late but I FINALLY found the time to repot my precious Bambi this weekend! It was my first time ever repotting-- it was a lot easier than I thought it would be, but definitely not the neatest process. It was very nice working with my hands once again though!


Got rid of a lot of the old soil, and she now has a lot more space to grow. A few days after the repotting I definitely can tell a difference I think. Less of her leaves have been falling off now and she looks a lot more green and lively. Yay!
